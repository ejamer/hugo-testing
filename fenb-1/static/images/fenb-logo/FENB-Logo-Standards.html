Fencing-Escrime NB:
Logo Standards Manual

Version 0.9 - April 2026
01 — Overview

Purpose

This manual establishes the standards for acceptable use of the Fencing-Escrime NB (FENB) logo, ensuring consistency in how our organization is represented across all materials and contexts.

These guidelines help guarantee a consistent and strong identity — a "brand" — for Fencing-Escrime NB. By managing our brand carefully, we create a presence that reflects the quality, integrity, and professionalism of fencing in New Brunswick. Please study these guidelines carefully and implement them immediately.

Written Approval Required: All clubs, partners, and external parties must obtain written approval from the Fencing-Escrime NB Executive Director before using the FENB logo in any capacity. See the Approval & Contact section for details.
Note: This manual does not address every possible graphic application. When in doubt about a specific use case, contact the Executive Director before proceeding.
02 — Overview

The Logo

The FENB logo is a single locked unit composed of two elements: the logomark (the fencer icon) and the logotype (the wordmark text). These must never be separated or used independently.

Logomark
The dynamic fencer icon, rendered in green and burgundy. Represents movement, precision, and the sport of fencing.
Logotype
FENCING / ESCRIME in green and NB in burgundy, set in Proxima Nova Condensed Black Italic.

Do not alter or recreate the logo. The logo's design, proportions, spacing, and colour relationships are fixed. Any deviation — however slight — diminishes the brand. Always use the official source files provided in this package.

It is unacceptable to scan the logo from reproduction materials or recreate it in any way.

03 — Identity

Logo Variations

The FENB logo is available in two layout configurations and three colour versions. Always choose the combination that provides the best legibility for the application.

Landscape Logo — Primary Configuration

The landscape logo is the primary configuration and should be used in most situations: websites, printed materials, documents, event signage, and anywhere horizontal space is available.

Full Colour Preferred
Use on white or light solid backgrounds. This is always the first choice.
White (One Colour)
Use on dark, green, burgundy, or strongly coloured backgrounds.
Black (One Colour)
For single-colour print: photocopying, faxing, embossing, engraving.

Small Logo — Compact Configuration

The small logo features the fencer icon and NB letterform only — without the FENCING / ESCRIME text. Use in contexts where space is limited or a square format is required: social media profile photos, app icons, badge applications, small merchandise items, and profile avatars.

Full Colour Preferred
Social media avatars, app icons, badges on light backgrounds.
White (One Colour)
Dark or coloured backgrounds in compact contexts.
Black (One Colour)
Single-colour print in compact contexts.

Provincial Team Element

The TEAM ÉQUIPE NB text element is used in conjunction with the FENB logo when representing New Brunswick at inter-provincial competitions. This element pairs with the FENB logo to form the complete provincial team identity for FENB members selected to represent the province outside of New Brunswick.

TEAM ÉQUIPE NB — Black
Paired with the FENB logo on light background materials.
TEAM ÉQUIPE NB — White
Paired with the FENB logo on dark background materials.

Additional Variations In Development

An Icon (standalone fencer logomark, without any text) and a Portrait (stacked layout) configuration are currently in development. These will be added to this package and standards manual upon completion.

04 — Identity

Colour Applications

The FENB logo may only be reproduced in the three approved colour versions.

Full Colour Primary
Green logomark and logotype with burgundy NB letterform, on a white or light background. This is always the preferred version — use it wherever possible.
White (One Colour)
All-white logo, for use on dark and strongly coloured backgrounds where the full-colour version would not be legible.
Black (One Colour)
All-black logo, reserved strictly for single-colour print applications: photocopying, faxes, embossing, engraving, and similar contexts where colour is unavailable.
No other colour versions are permitted. The logo must never be reproduced in any other colour or combination of colours not documented in this manual.
05 — Identity

Background Usage

Always ensure sufficient contrast between the logo and its background. Use the version that provides the best legibility for the specific context.

White Background
✓ Preferred
Full colour on white — always the first choice for any application.
Light Background
✓ Approved
Full colour on light grey or very light tones, provided contrast is sufficient.
Dark / Green Background
✓ Approved
Use the white version on dark, green, or strongly coloured backgrounds.
Clashing / Busy Background
✗ Never
Never place the logo on backgrounds that compromise legibility or clash with brand colours.
When using the logo over a photograph or graphic background, place it in a clear, uncluttered area of the image. If sufficient contrast cannot be achieved, add a solid white or dark colour panel behind the logo before placing it.

07 — Brand Elements

Typography

The FENB logo uses a single typeface. This font is an integral part of the logo's identity and must never be substituted or modified.

Typeface Proxima Nova
Weight Black (900)
Style Condensed Italic
Horizontal Skew −3.5°
Logo use only. Proxima Nova Condensed Black Italic is specified here solely as it relates to the logo artwork. This manual does not prescribe a body text or document typeface for general FENB communications.
08 — Usage Rules

Clear Space

A protected clear space zone around the FENB logo must be maintained at all times. This prevents other text, imagery, or graphic elements from crowding the logo and diminishing its impact.

Minimum clear space on all sides
= cap height of the "N" in NB

The minimum clear space on all four sides of the logo is equal to the cap height of the letter "N" in the NB logotype. This zone must remain completely free of all other text, graphic elements, imagery edges, and page trim lines.

This clear space requirement applies equally to all logo variations — landscape, small, and the TEAM ÉQUIPE NB Canada Games text element.
09 — Usage Rules

Minimum Size

The logo must never be reproduced below the minimum sizes listed below. Smaller sizes compromise the legibility of both the logomark and the logotype.

Print
1 inch
Minimum width for the landscape logo in any printed material. For portrait/stacked applications, minimum height is 0.75".
Digital / Web
75 px
Minimum width for on-screen use: websites, social media, digital advertising. For portrait/stacked applications, minimum height is 54 px.

For large-format applications — banners, vehicle graphics, event signage, trade show displays — always use one of the vector source files (SVG, EPS, AI, or PDF) to ensure the logo scales to any size without any loss of quality.

11 — Technical

File Formats

The FENB logo is supplied in multiple formats suited to different applications. Always use the most appropriate format for your context.

Format Type When to Use
.SVG Vector Websites and web applications. Fully scalable without any loss of quality. The preferred format for all digital use.
.EPS Vector Professional print production: stationery, signage, banners, brochures, promotional products, apparel, vinyl lettering. Send to print shops and professional designers.
.AI Vector Adobe Illustrator native file. For use by professional graphic designers working in the Adobe Creative Suite.
.PDF Vector Universal vector format viewable by anyone. Use when sharing with printers or partners who cannot open AI or EPS files.
.PNG Raster Internal documents, presentations (PowerPoint, Google Slides), email signatures, and website embedding. Transparent background. Use at the supplied size or smaller — never enlarge.
.JPG Raster Use only when a PNG or vector format is not accepted. No transparent background. Not suitable for professional print. Use at the supplied size or smaller — never enlarge.
Never enlarge a PNG or JPG. Scaling raster files up degrades image quality. For any application requiring a size larger than the supplied raster files, use a vector format (SVG, EPS, AI, or PDF) instead.

12 — Contact

Approval & Contact

Written approval from the Fencing-Escrime NB Executive Director is required before any club, media outlet, sponsor, partner, or external party uses the FENB logo in any public-facing material.

This requirement applies to — but is not limited to — apparel and merchandise, printed event materials, social media posts by third parties, websites and digital media, sponsorship and partner collateral, and any signage bearing the FENB name or mark.
